The Local Action Blueprint

A customizable, fillable framework that guides communities from lived experience to tangible civic action. Youth-led, trauma-informed, systems-aware, and implementation-ready.

Blueprint Characteristics

Youth-Led

Designed and driven by young people

Trauma-Informed

Built with care and psychological safety

Systems-Aware

Understanding interconnected impacts

Implementation-Ready

Practical and actionable from day one

Blueprint Structure

1

Context & Entry Point

Community context, entry pathway, SDG zone, stakeholders

2

Listening & Belonging Audit

Youth-led interviews, story circles, surveys, and creative methods surface lived experience, trust gaps, and informal systems.

Output: Belonging & Insight Brief
3

System Mapping

Fellows map decision pathways, communication flows, points of friction, and leverage points

Output: Visual systems maps
4

Dream → Design Translation

Youth articulate what must change and translate insight into protocols, policy concepts, tools, and pilot initiatives

5

Build & Pilot

Selected interventions are prototyped, tested, and refined with real stakeholders

6

Accountability & Learning Loop

Defines ownership, timelines, response expectations, and feedback mechanisms

7

Replication & Shared Learning

Insights are added to a shared repository so learning travels across cities

What Cities Receive

Pilot-Ready Solutions

Tested interventions and tools

Trained Youth Fellows

Skilled civic leaders ready to serve

Repeatable System

Infrastructure for ongoing civic innovation
