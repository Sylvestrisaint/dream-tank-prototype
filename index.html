The Heal–Dream–Build Framework

A structured framework for youth to respond thoughtfully to crisis and disruption, imagine better possibilities, and create tangible solutions that strengthen their communities.

In the aftermath of the gun violence that occurred on December 13, 2025, at Brown University, students, alumni, parents, and experts across disciplines came together. The conversations that followed revealed a broader need: a structured way to respond thoughtfully to crisis and disruption. While rooted in this specific moment, the framework is designed to be replicable across contexts, offering a structured way to respond to crisis wherever it occurs.

Goal

To enable youth to develop individual and collective resilience by producing a tangible civic or community-focused output, such as but not limited to a prototype, service, or structured proposal, within a short, defined timeframe using a repeatable Heal → Dream → Build process.

Why This Matters

A Crisis-Response Model for Rapid Recovery

Heal → Dream → Build is a crisis-response model that provides a structured yet empathetic pathway to rapid recovery and resilient solutions. It is not a separate system, but a faster mode of Dream Tank's Dream → Design → Launch framework, where priorities shift toward healing, coordination, and immediate action.

This matters because it prevents crises from being managed through speculation, misinformation, or purely top-down reactions, while also protecting younger participants from being overburdened. For cities, it means they are not building response systems in the middle of a crisis as trust, pathways, and roles already exist through this framework.

Moreover, the model activates a continuous feedback loop from youth to government. As a result, cities and campuses are able to respond with care, clarity, and speed rather than urgency alone.

The Process

A three-phase framework that transforms crisis into opportunity for growth and community building

HEAL

48–72 hour rapid listening

Rapid Listening & Community Support

Prioritize psychological safety, community gathering, and immediate needs assessment. Create spaces for processing, sharing, and identifying urgent challenges through trauma-informed approaches. This phase establishes trust and surfaces the most pressing concerns while ensuring everyone feels heard and supported. This work is conducted in partnership with licensed trauma professionals and city emergency planners, ensuring appropriate clinical oversight and alignment with established Resource Center response models. Youth participants are supported, not positioned as counselors, while trained professionals guide trauma response and care pathways.

DREAM

Collective Future Vision

Facilitate an adapted "Dream" phase focused on collective foresight and reimagining a more resilient future. Young people articulate needs and co-design solutions for the present crisis and future prevention. This collaborative visioning transforms raw emotion and experience into structured possibilities and actionable ideas.

BUILD

Rapid Prototyping & Action

Engage stakeholders in rapid prototyping and implementation of tangible solutions. Leverage youth insights to quickly develop and deploy interventions, focusing on immediate impact and adaptive learning. This phase turns vision into reality through concrete actions that address both urgent needs and long-term resilience.

Crisis Prevention

Trust Building

Creates continuous feedback loops between youth and institutional leadership

Misinformation Defense

Prevents speculation and rumor by establishing clear communication channels

Rapid Implementation

Trauma-Informed Care

Replicable Framework

Community Cohesion

Strengthens bonds through shared purpose and collaborative problem-solving
